# NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

## Overview

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) is a voluntary framework developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). It provides organizations with standards, guidelines, and best practices for managing cybersecurity risk.

The framework is flexible, scalable, and applicable to organizations of all sizes and industries.

---

## Purpose

The NIST CSF helps organizations:

- Identify cybersecurity risks
- Protect critical assets
- Detect security incidents
- Respond effectively to cyber attacks
- Recover from security events

---

## Core Functions

### Identify

Understand organizational assets, business environment, and cybersecurity risks.

Examples:

- Asset inventory
- Risk assessment
- Business context

---

### Protect

Implement safeguards to reduce cybersecurity risk.

Examples:

- Access control
- Security awareness training
- Data encryption
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

---

### Detect

Identify cybersecurity events as quickly as possible.

Examples:

- Security monitoring
- Log analysis
- Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)

---

### Respond

Take action during a cybersecurity incident.

Examples:

- Incident response plans
- Communication procedures
- Threat containment

---

### Recover

Restore operations after a security incident.

Examples:

- Disaster recovery
- Business continuity
- Lessons learned

---

## Benefits

- Improves cybersecurity maturity
- Provides a structured risk management approach
- Supports continuous improvement
- Flexible for organizations of different sizes
- Aligns security with business objectives

---

## Real-World Applications

Organizations use the NIST CSF to:

- Build cybersecurity programs
- Assess current security posture
- Improve risk management
- Support regulatory compliance
- Develop security roadmaps

---

## Example Scenario

A financial institution adopts the NIST CSF to strengthen its cybersecurity program.

The organization:

- Identifies critical assets.
- Implements MFA and encryption.
- Monitors security logs for suspicious activity.
- Activates its incident response plan during attacks.
- Restores services using disaster recovery procedures.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The NIST CSF is a voluntary cybersecurity framework.
- It helps organizations manage and reduce cybersecurity risk.
- Its five core functions provide a lifecycle approach to cybersecurity.
- It is one of the most widely adopted cybersecurity frameworks worldwide.
