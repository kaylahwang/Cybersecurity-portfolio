# Controls, Frameworks, and Compliance

## Overview

Organizations use security controls, frameworks, and compliance requirements together to manage cybersecurity risks and protect information assets.

These concepts are closely tied to the **CIA Triad**:

* **Confidentiality** – Prevent unauthorized access to information.
* **Integrity** – Ensure data remains accurate and trustworthy.
* **Availability** – Ensure systems and data are accessible when needed.

---

## Security Controls

Security controls are safeguards that reduce risks and protect systems from threats and vulnerabilities.

### Examples

* Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Access Control
* Encryption
* Security Monitoring

### Purpose

* Protect assets
* Reduce risk
* Support organizational security objectives

---

## Security Frameworks

Security frameworks provide structured guidance for building and maintaining cybersecurity programs.

### Core Components

1. Identify security goals
2. Establish policies and guidelines
3. Implement security processes and controls
4. Monitor and improve security performance

### Benefits

* Consistent security practices
* Improved risk management
* Better alignment with business objectives

---

## Compliance

Compliance refers to following internal policies and external regulations.

Organizations must comply with applicable laws, standards, and industry requirements to reduce legal, financial, and operational risks.

---

# Common Frameworks and Regulations

## NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

A voluntary framework developed by NIST to help organizations manage cybersecurity risks.

### Core Functions

* Identify
* Protect
* Detect
* Respond
* Recover

---

## NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)

Provides a structured approach to identifying, assessing, and managing security risks.

### Key Focus

* Risk assessment
* Security control implementation
* Continuous monitoring

---

## FERC-NERC

Applies to organizations involved in the North American power grid.

### Purpose

* Protect critical infrastructure
* Improve resilience against cyber threats
* Ensure incident reporting and response

---

## FedRAMP

A U.S. government program that standardizes security assessments for cloud service providers.

### Purpose

* Ensure consistent cloud security standards
* Support secure cloud adoption across federal agencies

---

## CIS Controls

A set of practical security best practices developed by the Center for Internet Security (CIS).

### Purpose

* Strengthen defenses
* Improve incident response readiness
* Reduce attack surface

---

## GDPR

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) protects the privacy and personal data of EU residents.

### Key Requirement

Organizations must notify affected individuals of certain data breaches within **72 hours**.

### Focus Areas

* Privacy
* Transparency
* Data protection

---

## PCI DSS

An international security standard for organizations that process, store, or transmit payment card information.

### Goal

Reduce credit card fraud and protect cardholder data.

---

## HIPAA

A U.S. law designed to protect patient health information (PHI).

### Core Rules

* Privacy Rule
* Security Rule
* Breach Notification Rule

### Related Framework

* HITRUST

---

## ISO

The International Organization for Standardization develops global standards across industries.

### Common Security Standard

* ISO/IEC 27001

### Benefits

* International recognition
* Improved governance
* Stronger risk management

---

## SOC Reports

SOC reports evaluate an organization's controls and risk management practices.

### Types

* SOC 1
* SOC 2

### Areas Assessed

* Security
* Availability
* Confidentiality
* Privacy
* Processing Integrity

---

# Key Takeaways

* Security controls reduce risks and protect assets.
* Security frameworks provide structured guidance for cybersecurity programs.
* Compliance ensures organizations meet legal and regulatory requirements.
* The CIA Triad remains the foundation of information security.
* Understanding major frameworks and regulations is essential for cybersecurity and GRC professionals.
